This map categorizes census tracts with Local Law 97-covered multifamily housing buildings into four quadrants by their level of rent burden and greenhouse gas emissions.

High-rent-burden tracts are defined as tracts where median gross rent is 30% or more of household income, and high-emission-tracts are defined as tracts where the median Greenhouse Gas (GHG) intensity is above Local Law 97's 2024 cap of 6.75 kg of CO2 per ft2.

While LL97 is critical to decarbonizing NYC's housing stock, tracts with both high rent burden and high emissions might be at risk of housing instability if property owners pass on compliance costs to already-burdened tenants. 22 City Council members have already signed onto a bill gutting LL97 in fear of housing affordability issues. This map can help ease this clash and direct more resources and supports to these "at risk" areas.
